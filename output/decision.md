## Why the other companies were not selected

### Corcept Therapeutics
Corcept is compelling, but too **binary** for the top choice.
- Its value is heavily tied to the **July 11, 2026 FDA PDUFA date** for relacorilant in platinum-resistant ovarian cancer.
- Positive approval could be transformative, but failure would be a major setback.
- The upside is high, but the near-term risk is also extremely high.

**Why not selected:** The company is attractive for catalyst traders, but the investment case is too dependent on one regulatory event, making it less reliable than Ligand’s diversified model.

### XOMA Royalty
XOMA has strategic value, but it is no longer the best standalone investment.
- It is already subject to a **$739 million acquisition by Ligand**
- The stock is primarily a **deal-arbitrage** situation
- Long-term independent upside is limited because the acquisition defines the outcome

**Why not selected:** XOMA is better viewed as a transaction than a long-term investment. Ligand captures the stronger post-deal opportunity.

### GSK
GSK is solid, but its growth profile is more moderate.
- It has scale, diversification, and a constructive oncology strategy
- The planned **Nuvalent acquisition** adds long-term value
- However, it is a mature large-cap pharma company with a slower growth profile than a more focused compounding platform

**Why not selected:** GSK is a good defensive healthcare holding, but Ligand offers a more attractive combination of growth and capital efficiency in this comparison.

### Allarity Therapeutics
Allarity is highly speculative and financially constrained.
- It is a **small-cap clinical-stage oncology company**
- Progress depends on stenoparib development and manufacturing milestones
- It carries meaningful **clinical risk, financing risk, and dilution risk**

**Why not selected:** The risk level is too high and the business is too fragile for a best-in-class investment choice.

### OSE Immunotherapeutics
OSE has encouraging clinical progress, but remains early-stage.
- It reported **positive topline TEDOVA Phase 2 results** for Tedopi in recurrent ovarian cancer
- That is a credible positive signal
- But it still needs larger confirmatory data and a clearer path to commercialization

**Why not selected:** The clinical story is promising, but it is still too early and uncertain compared with Ligand’s more mature and diversified investment case.
